Modern day surveillance is a tricky issue, the reason for this is the thin line between care and control. how much is too much surveillance? this mass data collection does not just collate information on what you do, instead it has broadened out to understand how you feel, your opinions and your moods. All of these factors are key for when shopping/ activities done throughout your day and it is all for 'purposes of influence, management, protection or direction'


Fingerprinting is a collection of information about a device for identification purposes. Client side scripting allows for very rich fingerprints. Through the use of JavaScript, fingerprinting can be used in a number of good and bad ways. For example, it can be used to combat fraud by checking the user is actually the real user not someone else. But it can also track users across websites without you knowing it, studying and monitoring your habits, creating this online persona of you.


Browser fingerprinting happens by visiting a web site, that site can generate a fingerprint, a unique identification to your computer. The fingerprint can then be sent to their websites server, and you can be tracked. No cookies required, no hacking, no “Do not track me” setting can make a difference just browsing the web will do it.


Even if you prevent sites from storing cookies on your computer, sites and ads can still track you. In fact, pretty much anybody can track you. In fact, you can pretty much guarantee right now on the web browsers are using fingerprinting to track you. Even if you disable Flash, you can still be tracked. If you disable JavaScript, it gets a lot harder. Most of the well-known techniques of browser fingerprinting are done via JavaScript however practically every web site on the planet requires JavaScript these days. JS is what makes Google Maps work, and Facebook, and Gmail, and everything else. Turn off JavaScript, and you’re pretty much browsing in the stone age. In short, you CAN’T turn off JS if you need to use anything on the internet.


As you may notice not all of the information above is technically useful for uniquely identifying someone (this is intentional), but it could be. It’s fairly basic info, but it’s great food for thought. There is a LOT more. When you put it all together, then it’s easy to see that with browser fingerprinting, you are absolutely unique and trackable on the internet.


It’s not the significance of ONE source of information from your browser that is valuable; it’s the combination of the bits of data.
